Project Gutenburg Overview

Project Gutenburg was begun by Michael Hart in 1971 in order to produce replicable electronic texts to be shared and used by a large audience. It aims to provide open-access literature at 99.9% accuracy to a broad audience with access to diverse browsing technologies.

The etexts are transcribed in the simplest text form in order that they can be read and replicated easily.

History of Project Gutenburg

With $100,000,000 in computer time through his operator account, Hart decided that the greatest value of computers would be the storage, retrieval, searching of information. He created an e-text of the Declaration of Independence, and argued that this equalled the vast amount of money in computer time because the e-text accounted for 100,000,000 accessible copies of the Declaration of Independence. From there, the project grew to include fifty years worth of open access literature in replicable, accessible e-text format.

Database Content

The project provides a variety of etext formats for books. Each item includes a list of descriptive elements, including:

  • Biographical information
  • Copyright status
  • Release date
  • Reading level

Some data points, such as author, are linked to more texts by the same author, or to related books.

Users are able to search books with advanced search tools, or the database can generate a list of books at random from which the user can choose. The website also arranges books by:

  • Alphabet
  • Popularity
  • Language
  • Date of publication within the Gutenburg Project

The collection has over 75,000 titles and it continually grows as materials become open-source.

Unique Features

Project Gutenburg's versatile texts can be read online, downloaded and printed, or transfered to a handheld device to be read through applications such as Kindle.

Collection Users

Project Gutenburg is an open source for anyone with access to the internet. It can be used by researchers or anyone interested in reading literature in the public domain.

Drawbacks to Collection

Project Gutenburg's collection is limited by the following factors:

  • Not authorized to publish materials protected by copyright
  • Publishes only one edition of an open source text
  • Does not necessarily provide the most authoritative version of a text

While the collection cannot provide open-source e-texts for all literary works, it provides easy access to a broad range of literature for academic or leisurely consumption.

Conclusion

The utility of this resources cannot be understated. Whether you are reading for class, for research, or for fun, Project Gutenburg is an excellent resource for discovering, accessing, and enjoying literature in the public domain. As long as you have access to the Internet, you have have over 70,000 titles at your fingertips.
